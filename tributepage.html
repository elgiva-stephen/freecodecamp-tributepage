Buchi Emecheta-Writer

Florence Onyebuchi Emecheta OBE (21 July 1944 - 25 January 2017) was a Nigerian born novelist based in the UK. She also wrote plays, articles, an autobiography and childrens' books. She authored over 20 books, including Second Class Citizen (1974).

Emecheta's theme of child slavery, motherhood, female independence and freedom through education gained recognition from critics and honors. Her works explore the tension between tradition and modernity and has been characterized as the first successful black woman novelist living in Britain after 1948.

Timeline of Buchi Emecheta's Life

  • 1944 - Born in Lagos, Nigeria
  • 1953 - Lost her father to complications from an injury sustained in the swamps of Burma
  • 1960 - Got married at 16 to a boy she was engaged to when she was 11
  • 1962 - Moved to London with her two children
  • 1966 - Left her husband while pregnant with her fifth child
  • 1972 - Earned a degree in Sociology from the University of London and published her first book In the Ditch
  • 1974 - Published Second Class Citizen
  • 1976 - Released the The Brideprice
  • 1977 - Released the The Slave Girl
  • 1979 - Released the The Joys of Motherhood and won the Joel Campell award and a member of the British Home Secretary's Advisory Council on rape
  • 1981 - Released the The Moonlight Bride
  • 1982 - Released the Destination Biafra
  • 1983 - Released the The rape of Shavi
  • 1986 - Released the A kind of Marriage and became a Fellow at the University of London
  • 1989 - Released the Gwendolen
  • 1994 - Released the Kehinde
  • 2000 - Released the The New Tribe
  • 2010 - Suffered a stroke
  • 2017 - Died at the age of 72

Most of Buchi Emecheta's works are focused on sexual discrimination and racial prejudice informed by her own experiences as both a single mother and a black woman living in the United Kingdom.
